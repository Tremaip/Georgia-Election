Biden Wins Georgia with the Help of Georgia's Blackest Counties

Written By: Tremain Prioleau II

December 11th, 2020

Image Source: NYMAG.com

In an unprecedented election year with twists and turns at every corner, Georgia has emerged as the lone Blue state of the South granting president-elect Joe Biden its 16 electoral votes.

Georgia is notoriously known as a safe Red state where Republicans easily win elections. Biden is the first Democrat to win Georgia since Clinton in 1992. Much of Democrat’s success in Georgia comes from some of it’s more heavily diverse counties.

The state’s population makeup has changed in recent years due to an influx of Latino, Asian, and Black residents in counties like Gwinnett, Fulton, Cobb and Dekalb. But much of this win can be attributed to Black voters and organizers working in these communities. Maurice Mitchell, National director of the Working Families Party and Movement for Black Lives Strategist says, “We believed in the power of Black voters and Black organizers in our movement.” Their work propelled Biden to victory in these counties.
